3D Ferrofluid Simulation

In this project, we will implement a standard particle based 3D fluid simulation, and then add on the capability to simulate ferrofluids, which are fluids that are attracted to magnetic poles. Additionally, we will use Blender to render the particles as a smooth fluid.

Andrew Wang, Eshaan Bhansali, Nate Tausik, Larry Yan

Problem Description

Our main problem is simulating fluid particles with magnetic properties. The problem is important because it will allow us to gain a better understanding of properties of ferrofluid. We expect rendering the fluid particles to be challenging due to lack of Blender experience. To solve this problem, we will first learn how the particles in a fluid behave and interact with each other using the Navier-Stokes equations. After implementing the basic fluid simulation, we will add the specific properties of the ferrofluid’s interaction with magnetic fields. Then we will learn how to use Blender to render the particles using online resources and previous projects. We will try to implement the distinct look of the ferrofluid’s reflective properties in the rendering stage.

Goals and Deliverables

We would like to create a short video displaying the properties of the fluid and its interaction with magnetic fields.

Example of what we’re trying to simulate: https://youtu.be/GSaipfkGpVs?t=103 (Timestamped)

We will measure the quality of our project mainly by comparing it to the behavior of real ferrofluids or existing ferrofluid simulations. We will measure the performance by simply timing the rendering time.

With our analysis, we will try to answer questions such as how the viscosity, surface tension, and magnetic field strength affects the behavior of the ferrofluid.

  1. We plan to deliver at least a proper simulation of the fluid particles. We plan to simulate how the fluid particles interact with each other, with a gravitational field, and finally with a magnetic field as well. We will produce a video showing how the fluid particles respond to different types of magnetic fields both with and without gravity, and in different types of containers.

  1. We will hope to deliver a rendering of the fluid on top of that. This would convert a simulation of just the particle positions to a more realistic visualization of a real ferrofluid, and produce a more visually appealing, realistic looking, demonstration of the characteristics of the ferrofluid. We also hope to give even more examples of the ferrofluid. For instance, we could have the fluid fall onto a magnet, or into a magnetic container, or even have a changing magnetic field.
